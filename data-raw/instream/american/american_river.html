Instream Spawning and Rearing Habitat

Data Source: ARWA 2017 and USFWS 1985.

Instream spawning habitat for Fall Run Chinook Salmon and Steelhead in the American River is based on the spawning habitat suitability and availability analysis in the Lower American River Biological Rationale Development and Performance of the Modified Flow Standard (ARWA 2017). Instream juvenile rearing habitat for Fall Run Chinook Salmon is based on instream flow studies condicted by Mark Gard from the U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service. Both evaluations developed relationships between flow and suitable spawning and rearing habitat for the Lower American River.

Spawning WUA

The Fall Run Chinook Salmon and Steelhead spawning habitat data from ARWA 2017 is contained in Attachment B, Table B-4 on page 91 and Figure B-8 on page 92 (Fall Run Chinook Salmon) and Attachment B, Table B-9 and Figure B-26 on page 107 (Steelhead).

Rearing WUA

The fry and juvenile instream rearing habitat weighted usable areas for Fall Run Chinook Salmon in the American River provided by Mark Gard (see ~/cvpiaHabitat/data-raw/mark_gard_data/IFIMWUA.xlsx). The values contained in cells A35:D46 of the American spreadhseet tab are the sums of the values from the instream flow evaluation reaches called Sailor Bar, Ancil Hoffman, Watt, and H Street. These values were divided by four to get composite instream rearing weighted usable area values in square feet per 1000 feet of river.
